Discrimination and/or Harrassment in the Workforce

The Equal Opportunity Commission (EEOC) was put in place to enforce federal laws that prohibit employment discrimination.

Examples of such discrimination include:
  • Race
  • Color
  • Religion
  • Sex
  • Pregnancy
  • Transgender Status
  • Sexual Orientation
  • National Origin
  • Age (40 or Older)
  • Disability (or Genetic Information
*Applies to every type of work situations (hiring, firinf, promotions, harassment, training, wages, and benefits)
The EEOC has the authority to investigate discrimination charges against employers covered by the law. The law works to prevent discrimination before it happens through outreach, education, and technical assistance programs.

Voter Accessibility Laws

The Voter accessibility laws ensure that people with disabilities or language barriers are able to vote.

The Help America Vote Act (HAVA) protects the voting rights of individuals with disabilities, such as:
  • The right to vote in private (without help, have an accessible polling place (with voting machines specifically for voters with disabilities)

  • Polling places must have wheelchair-accessible voting booths, entrances and doorways (at least 32 inches wide), handrails on all stairs, special equipment for voters who are blind or visually impaired

  • If you have a disability, you may seek help from poll workers trained to use an accessible voting machine or bring someone to help you vote

  • You can also as your local election office what your other options like te possibility of "curbside voting" (when a poll worker brings everything you need to vote in your vehicle), transportation to the polls, and voting by mail
